Attention-Driven Alaryngeal Speech Enhancement via Discrete Representation Learning and Timbre-Preserving Augmentation

Ziqian Wang, Xinfa Zhu, Zihan Zhang, Yuanjun Lv, Ning Jiang, Guoqing Zhao, Lei Xie
Audio, Speech and Language Processing Group (ASLP@NPU) School of Computer Science,

Northwestern Polytechnical University, Xi’an, China
Mashang Consumer Finance Co., Ltd.

Paper arXiv

Abstract

Alaryngeal speech enhancement seeks to restore intelligibility and naturalness for individuals who have undergone laryngectomy. Existing approaches based on direct spectral mapping or frame-level voice conversion fail to exploit the linguistic structure underlying speech and are constrained by scarce pathological data. We propose a novel multi-stage framework integrating (i) discrete representation learning and (ii) attention-driven feature alignment. In the first stage, WavLM embeddings are quantized into discrete tokens via k-means clustering and refined by a bidirectional Speech Language Model that maps pathological token patterns toward normal speech, followed by Conformer-based reconstruction of continuous features. In the second stage, an Attention-Driven Feature Matcher (ADFM) further enhances the reconstructed features through three tightly coupled modules: a residual Feature Enhancer with learnable scaling, a Multi-Head Attention Matcher that performs soft alignment over k-nearest normal-speech neighbors, and a Quality Predictor that estimates match confidence to adaptively weight each feature. A HiFi-GAN vocoder synthesizes the final waveform. To overcome data scarcity, we further introduce a Multi-Mask GAN augmentation strategy extending MaskCycleGAN-VC with a timbre similarity loss for speaker identity preservation. Evaluations on Thai and English corpora show that our system achieves the best speaker similarity in both languages (0.858 Thai, 0.850 English) while simultaneously achieving competitive or state-of-the-art FAD (5.252 Thai, 5.385 English) and MCD (5.644 Thai, 5.322 English), with up to 27.3% FAD reduction over kNN-VC. Subjective tests confirm significant listener preference for the enhanced speech (77% English, 67% Thai; p<0.001).
